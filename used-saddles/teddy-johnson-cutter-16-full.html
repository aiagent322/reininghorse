About This Saddle

This Teddy Johnson Cutter carries the brand stamp of Teddy Johnson Cutting Horses, Gainesville, Texas — a name with deep roots in the Texas cutting horse community. The saddle features deep-relief full floral tooling throughout, a dark brown smooth leather seat, sterling silver conchos, and a woolskin underside for horse comfort.

Notably, the fenders carry an NCHA $30,000 AMT (Average Money Total) stamp — a designation that indicates this saddle has documented competition history at the National Cutting Horse Association level. For the cutting horse enthusiast, that provenance adds meaningful context to an already solid piece of craftsmanship.

The deep cantle and high swells are characteristic of the cutting saddle design — built to hold the rider secure during a horse's independent cow work. At $1,195 this is an exceptional value for a competition-pedigreed cutting saddle with this level of tooling and provenance. Certified by David Solum.
